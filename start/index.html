Portrait of Eleanor of Toledo

The Portrait of Eleanor of Toledo and Her Son is a painting by the Italian artist Agnolo di Cosimo, known as Bronzino, finished ca. 1545. One of his most famous works, it is housed in the Uffizi Gallery of Florence, Italy and is considered one of the preeminent examples of Mannerist portraiture.

Portrait of Artist

Portrait of the Artist Holding a Thistle is an oil painting on parchment pasted on canvas by German artist Albrecht Durer, painted in 1493. It is the earliest of Durer's painted self-portraits and has been identified as on of the first self-portraits painted by a Northern artist.

Girl with a Pearl Earring

Girl with a Pearl Earring is one of Johannes Verrneer's masterworks and , as the name implies, uses a pearl earring for the focal point. Today the painting is kept in the Mauritshuis gallery in The Hague. It is sometimes referred to as "the Mona Lisa of the North" or "the Duth Mona Lisa".
